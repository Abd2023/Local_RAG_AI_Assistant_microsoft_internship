# Northstar AI Summer School Support And Policies

Students should ask for help when they are blocked for more than 30 minutes. The preferred support path is first to ask a teammate, then check the course notes, then ask the instructor during lab time. This order helps students practice debugging while still preventing long delays.

Office hours are held every Tuesday and Thursday from 4:15 PM to 5:00 PM. Office hours are used for conceptual questions, environment problems, and project design reviews. Students do not need an appointment for regular office hours.

The course uses a shared issue log for common setup problems. When a team finds a new error, they should record the command they ran, the error message, what they expected to happen, and what fixed the issue. This issue log helps other teams avoid repeating the same debugging work.

Students may use AI assistants for explanation, planning, and debugging support. They must not submit code they cannot explain. During the demo, the instructor may ask a team member to explain how chunking, retrieval, or prompt construction works in their own project.

Private data should not be placed in the sample knowledge base. The first dataset should use fictional or public information only. Teams that later use real documents must confirm that the documents are allowed to be used in a local AI demo.

The course does not provide grades for attendance alone. Attendance supports learning, but the final assessment depends on the working assistant, clear documentation, thoughtful evaluation, and the ability to explain design decisions.
