# Northstar AI Summer School Schedule And Attendance

The standard daily schedule starts with a 15-minute standup at 10:00 AM. Each team states what they completed yesterday, what they plan to do today, and whether they are blocked. The instructor uses this standup to identify teams that need extra help.

The morning lesson runs from 10:15 AM to 12:00 PM. On Mondays and Tuesdays, the lesson usually introduces a new concept such as chunking, embeddings, vector similarity, or prompt design. On Wednesdays and Thursdays, the lesson focuses on implementation patterns and debugging techniques.

Lunch break is from 12:00 PM to 1:00 PM. Afternoon project lab runs from 1:00 PM to 3:30 PM. The final 30 minutes of the day are used for progress notes, code cleanup, and a short written reflection.

Attendance is required for at least 85 percent of scheduled sessions. A student who misses more than three full days must meet with the instructor to create a catch-up plan. Late arrivals of more than 20 minutes count as a partial absence.

The first project checkpoint is at the end of Week 1, when teams must show that their Python environment and local model test are working. The second checkpoint is at the end of Week 2, when teams must show a working embedding demo. The third checkpoint is at the end of Week 3, when teams must show document chunks stored in SQLite.

Final demos happen on the last Friday of Week 4. Each demo should last five minutes and include one answerable question, one unanswerable question, and a short explanation of which document chunks were retrieved.
